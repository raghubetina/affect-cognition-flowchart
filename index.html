Affect-Cognition Flowchart

This interactive flowchart turns Gerald L. Clore and Alexander J. Schiller's chapter on the affect-cognition connection into a decision aid: given a mood or emotion manipulation, what kind of effect should you expect, and why? It is a synthesis of the chapter's logic rather than a figure reproduced from the book, so it works best as a heuristic for tracing likely effects. Its core idea is the affective immediacy principle: feelings are experienced as being about whatever is most salient in mind at the time.

How to use it: start at the top and follow the branch that best matches the method you are thinking about. Every node in the diagram is clickable. Click a node to open a modal with a fuller explanation, the key examples or case studies, and the supporting textbook page numbers.
